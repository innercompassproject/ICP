What ICP names precisely
Pillar 2 — VMV
"Your VMV doesn't resolve hard decisions."
Your values document exists. When the team faces a genuinely difficult trade-off, they come to you — not because the values are wrong, but because they are too abstract to use. The gap between declared values and operational values is the actual culture. It is visible to everyone except, frequently, leadership.
Pillar 6 — Financial Intelligence
"You don't know your real margin without looking."
You know revenue. You feel profitable. You cannot state the margin on your core product without opening a spreadsheet. That is not financial intelligence — it is financial exposure. Decisions are being made without knowing the actual position.
Pillar 3 — Leadership Architecture
"Your team waits for you."
Every significant decision returns to you. This feels like leadership. It is structural centralisation — a ceiling placed on every person in the organisation. The business's maximum capacity is your personal bandwidth. That is a design failure, not a people failure.
Pillar 7 — Customer Clarity
"You serve 'everyone' — which means nobody precisely."
Your customer is a demographic. "SMEs in Southeast Asia" is a market, not a person. There is no specific, known pain your most loyal buyer would pay to solve — at least not written down and operationally applied. This imprecision makes every downstream decision slower and harder.

Before you apply

This is not light.
Read this before submitting.

This is the right path if
  • You're willing to be uncomfortable. Structural diagnosis surfaces things founders would rather not see. That is the purpose.
  • You'll do the implementation work — not just receive the documents, but act on them across six months.
  • You have a real business with real customers and real decisions being made. ICP does not produce useful output on pre-revenue ideas.
  • You think in systems. ICP works at the structural level, not the tactical. If you're looking for campaign templates, this is not the journey.
  • You're ready to name what's actually wrong — not what you'd prefer to be wrong.
This is not the right path if
  • You want validation of decisions already made. ICP will not provide that.
  • You're looking for immediate tactical outputs — a new website, a campaign, a rebrand.
  • You want a light advisory touch. This requires active participation across 6 months.
  • Your business is pre-revenue or early ideation. The kits require a real operating context.
If you are looking for community, mentorship, or emotional support — this is not that. ICP is a structural diagnostic tool. It is not a peer network, not a coaching relationship, not a safe space for processing. It is rigorous, bilateral, and honest about what it is and isn't.
