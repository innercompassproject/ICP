DRIFT
The problem

A venture can be
growing and losing
its foundation
at the same time.

The problem is never effort.
It is always structure.

Every venture starts with clarity. The founder sees something others don't. A team forms around a shared belief. Growth begins — revenue increases, the team expands, opportunities appear everywhere.

Effort is not the problem. Structure is.

And slowly, almost invisibly, something shifts. Decisions become reactive. Trade-offs become transactional. The original purpose becomes words on a wall instead of a filter for real choices. The organisation is working harder. But it is no longer pointed in the right direction.

That is drift. It is not a sudden crisis. It is a structural problem that builds quietly, year on year, until the gap between where the business is and where it was built to go is very hard to close.

"The problem is never effort. Most founders are working harder than ever — with no clear picture of what is specifically broken, and no path to fix it in the right order."
This is not a training programme. It is a standard.

The Inner Compass Project is built as a long-term response to this structural problem. Not a consultancy. Not coaching. Not a movement. An operating standard — specific enough to tell any founder, in any country, exactly what is missing from their foundation and what to build first.

ICP works not because it invented new principles. It works because it names what already exists in every business that has lasted — and makes those principles accessible to founders who have never had a complete structural map before.
