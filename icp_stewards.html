The Constraint

Why five.
Not fifteen.

Five is not an arbitrary number. It is a structural decision made for a specific reason: a standard is only as reliable as the integrity of the people building it.

ISO does not invite everyone to the founding committee. The constraint is the quality control. More stewards would mean more coordination, more dilution of voice, more noise in the evidence base. Five people who are fully committed produce better structural material than fifteen who are partially involved.

If more people apply than there are seats, the excess applications are a signal — not a problem to solve by adding seats. Scarcity here is not a sales mechanism. It is an architectural decision.

Founding Steward Seats — All Available
All five seats remain open. Each seat is confirmed personally after a bilateral conversation. Selection is based on fit with the build phase, not credentials.
The Structural Reasons
Standards require signal clarity
The case studies stewards generate will directly inform what v1.0 says. Five voices, carefully chosen, produce cleaner signal than a larger group with variable commitment levels.
Attribution dilutes as contributors scale
Five founding stewards each carry meaningful attribution weight. At fifteen, the founding contribution becomes a footnote. The limit protects the value of the seat itself.
The build phase has finite coordination capacity
At v0.4, with kits still in development and the implementor base forming, the build cannot absorb more than five contributing architects without structural noise. This is honest about current capacity.
More applied will not mean more seats
If ten serious people apply, five will be declined. The constraint does not flex based on demand. If this feels arbitrary, re-read the ISO analogy. Committees have limits for a reason.
12-month review is the natural capacity checkpoint
After Phase 1, the structure of the steward relationship is formally reviewed. Additional contributors may be appropriate at that stage. Not before. The build earns the right to scale.

The conversation
happens first.

Applications are not processed and responded to. They are read personally. If your application makes a case for the seat, a short conversation follows — not an interview, a bilateral assessment. You are evaluating the build. We are evaluating the fit.

Not everyone who applies will be invited to that conversation. Not everyone who has that conversation will be offered a seat. This is structural, not personal.

  • 1
    Submit the application
    Three questions. Specific answers. Not a cover letter — a diagnostic of your fit. Read carefully before writing.
  • 2
    Personal review
    Every application is read. Responses are sent within 7 days. If the application does not advance, you will be told clearly — not left waiting.
  • 3
    Bilateral conversation
    A 30-minute call. You ask what you need to ask about the build. We assess whether the seat fits you at this stage of the project.
  • 4
    Seat confirmation
    If both sides agree, the seat is confirmed. Phase 1 begins immediately. No probation period — the confirmation is the commitment.
