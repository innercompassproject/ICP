25-year architecture

Where Business Compass
sits in the larger picture.

ICP is not one product. It is a five-pillar operating architecture designed to compound over 25 years. Only Pillar 1 is active. The remaining four are deliberately deferred ‚Äî not because of resource constraints, but because building them before Pillar 1 has proof would produce five weak foundations instead of one strong one.

Decision log

Seven decisions that
shaped the build.

Major build decisions are documented here with the reasoning. Not to justify them ‚Äî to show how the thinking actually works, and to make it possible to revisit a decision if the evidence changes.

üó∫
Decision: Build one pillar before five
The original ICP architecture spans five pillars across a 25-year horizon. The decision to build only Pillar 1 first ‚Äî and defer Pillars 2‚Äì5 explicitly ‚Äî was made in late 2025. The reasoning: attempting to build five pillars simultaneously with limited capital produces five weak foundations. One pillar built with proof produces the evidence base that makes every subsequent pillar possible. The 25-year arc guides direction. Capital discipline guides sequence.
Decided: Q4 2025
üë•
Decision: 50 founders before 500
ICP's 2026 goal is 500 founders. The decision was made to structure the first phase around 50 Founding Implementors ‚Äî not 500 immediately ‚Äî because 50 completed journeys with documented outcomes is a more valuable proof than 500 partial ones. The Founding Rate exists to attract founders who will complete the process, not just begin it. Quality of evidence over volume of access.
Decided: Feb 2026
üí∞
Decision: Founding Rate, not subsidised pricing
The $500/month, 6-month Founding Rate was deliberately framed as a partnership ‚Äî access at the rate that reflects where the standard is today (v0.4), not where it is going ($15K at v1.0). The word "subsidised" was explicitly rejected ‚Äî it positions the early price as charity rather than as a fair exchange for shaping something not yet proven. Founders at the Founding Rate are co-investors in the evidence base, not discounted customers.
Decided: Feb 2026
üå±
Decision: Five steward seats, not an open cohort
The Steward model uses five named seats rather than an open practitioner cohort. The reasoning: five people who are deeply engaged produce better standard refinement than fifty who are loosely affiliated. The credibility of the standard depends on the quality of the field evidence ‚Äî and quality requires structured commitment from a small group, not broad participation from a large one. Seats expand after Phase 1 if the model is proven.
Decided: Feb 2026
üèó
Decision: API-first MVP, not a platform
The technology approach uses the Anthropic API and a minimal interface ‚Äî deliberately avoiding the complexity of a full SaaS platform until there is real usage data from founders. Building infrastructure before understanding how founders actually use the kit system would produce expensive wrong answers. The MVP delivers the journey at quality for 50 founders. Platform decisions get made from that evidence, not before it.
Decided: Jan 2026
üåç
Decision: Terra Bali as the initial proving ground
The first cohort of Founding Implementors is being built through direct engagement with business owners in the context of Terra Bali and direct founder relationships in the region. This is not a market access strategy ‚Äî it is an evidence-building strategy. The goal is documented journeys from real businesses in real geographies ‚Äî not geographic expansion for its own sake. The standard will be stronger for being tested in specific, constrained contexts before being generalised.
Decided: Q4 2025
üìñ
Decision: Build publicly, document everything
This page ‚Äî the public build log ‚Äî is itself a decision. Transparency about how ICP is built, what decisions were made, and what is deferred is not a marketing tactic. It is the mechanism by which institutions will eventually trust the standard enough to adopt it. An organization willing to document its reasoning publicly is an organization that can be held accountable. That accountability is what institutional relationships require.
Decided: Feb 2026

What the build reveals

Four things we expect
to learn by end 2026.

The build is not just about producing a standard. It is about discovering what the standard needs to say that it cannot know yet ‚Äî without real founders going through it.

Which pillar breaks first ‚Äî and why
Of the seven Foundation Pillars, one or two will consistently be the most structurally broken in the businesses going through the journey. That pattern is information. It will reshape which pillar the kit system leads with, and which gets the most detailed treatment in v1.0.
Where the kits need to go deeper
Some kits will produce outputs founders find immediately useful. Others will produce outputs that feel right but don't hold up in real decisions. The gap between those two outcomes is the most valuable refinement signal the field can give us.
What "completion" actually requires
The design assumes founders who engage seriously will complete the journey. Reality will show what commitment level produces completion, what gets founders stuck, and what the orientation call needs to screen for to ensure the right founders are entering the journey.
Whether the Founding Rate attracts the right founders
The Founding Rate model assumes that price-as-partnership attracts founders who will complete the journey and produce high-quality evidence. If the conversion rate from orientation call to completed journey is low, the pricing or framing needs to change. The model is a hypothesis. 50 journeys tests it.
