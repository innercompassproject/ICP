25-year architecture

The arc.
Four phases. One direction.

It is a slow build toward a standard designed to outlast its founders. Each phase depends on what the previous phase proves. Patience is not a weakness here. It is the structural principle.

Standing at the base of a cathedral under construction, you do not see the finished spire. You see foundations being laid with precision, each stone placed to bear the weight of everything above it. The visible work is always the least dramatic part. The invisible structure is what makes the rest possible.
1
Phase 1 · 2025–2026
Proof
Build Pillar 1. Get 50 founders through the complete kit journey. Document real outcomes. Have founding stewards sign off on all 11 pillars as field-tested. Earn v1.0. This phase is the load-bearing foundation everything else rests on.
2
Phase 2 · 2027–2030
Transmission
Extend the standard into education and practitioner development. Build formal training for people who will carry the methodology into communities that cannot be reached directly. The architecture transmits through people, not just through a platform.
3
Phase 3 · 2030–2040
Infrastructure
Institutional bridges — with governments, development bodies, and foundations whose programs reach the MSMEs ICP is built to serve. This phase is not about expansion. It is about structural integration into the systems that already reach scale.
4
Phase 4 · 2040+
Regeneration
Ecosystem-level transformation — when the standard has sufficient institutional presence to begin reshaping the conditions that produce structural failure in the first place. This is the arc's horizon. It is what Phase 1 is making possible.
"The 25-year horizon is not ambition. It is constraint." Building something that outlasts its founders requires resisting the pressure to move faster than the evidence supports. Phase 1 is the proof. Everything else is what proof makes possible.
5-pillar system

Where Business Compass
sits in the larger structure.

Five structural pillars. Each one dependent on the previous. Only the first is active — not because the others are unimportant, but because each must earn its right to be built. Deferral is discipline. Not neglect.

05
Deferred · 2031+
Global Sustainability
  • Strategic FunctionInstitutional engagement with the structural conditions — policy, capital, trade — that determine the environment MSMEs operate in.
  • Structural DependencyRequires Pillars 1–4 proven and institutional relationships from Phase 3 infrastructure already established.
  • First SignalThe first policy brief produced from ICP evidence, submitted to a government body or development finance institution.
Why the sequence is not negotiable: Building Pillar 2 before Pillar 1 has proof produces an education program with no evidence base. Building Pillar 3 before Pillar 2 produces community programs with no trained practitioners. Each layer must be load-bearing before the next layer is placed on it. This is structural logic, not caution.
Why this moment matters

2026 is the year the standard is shaped
before it hardens.

Standards are not built once and fixed. They are built, tested, refined — and then, at some point, their architecture becomes sufficiently established that major structural change requires dismantling what's already been built on top of it. That point is v1.0. Before v1.0, the standard is still being formed. The people inside that formation — founding implementors, founding stewards — directly influence what the standard becomes. After v1.0, that window closes.
01
Structural flexibility is highest now. Every pillar definition is still being refined through field use. A founding steward can change how Pillar 4 is defined. After v1.0, changing a pillar definition requires dismantling what's been built on top of it.
02
Attribution is permanent. Founding contributors are named in Standard v1.0. That naming is a permanent record — in every institutional conversation, every case study, every future pillar that references the foundation they helped shape.
03
The Founding Rate closes when 50 journeys are complete. The rate that reflects where the standard is today — v0.4, unproven — exists only until the evidence base is built. After that, the rate reflects what the standard has demonstrated.

Decision log

Key decisions.
With the reasoning.

Major build decisions are documented here — not to justify them, but to show how the thinking works and to make it possible to revisit a decision if the evidence changes. Institutional observers deserve to see this reasoning.

🏗
Build one pillar before five
Attempting to build all five pillars simultaneously with limited capital produces five weak foundations. One pillar built with proof produces the evidence base — and institutional credibility — that makes every subsequent pillar possible. The 25-year arc guides direction. Capital discipline guides sequence. Ambition without sequencing is aspiration. This is not that.
Decided Q4 2025
👥
50 founders before 500
50 completed journeys with documented outcomes is more valuable than 500 partial ones. The Founding Rate exists to attract founders who will complete the process, not just begin it. Quality of evidence over volume of access — the 500 goal comes after the 50 prove the model. Evidence compounds. Partial evidence does not.
Decided Feb 2026
🔧
No community layer
Community infrastructure — forums, peer channels, group programming — requires maintenance and moderation that diverts from building the standard itself. ICP is a structural tool. If connection between founders emerges naturally from shared use of the standard, that is a consequence, not a feature. A community layer that competes with the standard for attention is a structural mistake at this stage.
Decided Feb 2026
⏱
Kit interface not rushed
The prompt architecture is complete. The interface is in build. These are distinct stages — and the interface launch is not being compressed. A poorly deployed interface undermines trust in the methodology itself. The kits will open when the interface is structurally ready. Not when a launch date demands it. This decision is revisited only if a structurally sound shortcut emerges — not because of pressure.
Decided Feb 2026
💰
Founding Rate, not discounted pricing
The $500/month rate is framed as a partnership — access at the rate that reflects where the standard is today (v0.4), not where it is going. "Subsidised" was explicitly rejected. That word positions founders as beneficiaries of charity. Founding Implementors are structural partners contributing to the evidence base that makes v1.0 possible. The rate reflects that honestly.
Decided Feb 2026
🌱
Five steward seats, not an open cohort
Five people deeply engaged produce better standard refinement than fifty loosely affiliated. The credibility of the standard depends on the quality of the field evidence — and quality requires structured commitment from a small group. Dilution destroys standards. Steward seats expand after Phase 1 if the model is proven. Not before. The constraint is not a limitation — it is the mechanism.
Decided Feb 2026
📖
Build publicly, document everything
Transparency about how ICP is built — what decisions were made and what was deferred — is not a marketing tactic. It is the mechanism by which institutions will eventually trust the standard enough to adopt it. An organisation willing to document its reasoning publicly is one that can be held accountable. That accountability is load-bearing.
Decided Feb 2026
